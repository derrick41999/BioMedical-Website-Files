1.2 Information Collected Automatically

Usage Information. Whenever you visit or interact with the Site, we, as well as any third-party service providers, may use a variety of technologies that automatically or passively collect information about how the Site is accessed and used (“Usage Information”). Usage Information may include your browser type and version, operating system, screen resolution, the page served, the time, and your use of features on the Site. Device Identifier. We may collect your IP address or other unique identifier (“Device Identifier”) for the Device (computer, mobile phone, tablet or other device) you use to access the Site. A Device Identifier is a number that is assigned to your Device when you access a website or its servers, and our computers identify your Device by its Device Identifier. We may use a Device Identifier to, among other things, administer the Site, diagnose problems with our servers, evaluate Site navigation and better understand the demographics of our Site visitors so we can provide more relevant content, functionality and offers. If we collect any Device Identifiers on the Site, we will only have access to the country identifier portion of the Device Identifier. The portion of the Device Identifier that is personally identifiable to you will be masked. We will not be able to personally identify you based on the masked Device Identifier.

Cookies. Cookies are data files placed on a Device when it is used to visit the Site (“Cookies”). We may use Cookies to collect and store Usage Information, for security purposes, and to facilitate site navigation. Cookies can remain in your computer for the duration of a browsing session or for a predetermined period of time. Cookies that remain in your computer for the duration of a browsing session are called "session" cookies. Cookies that remain in your computer for a predetermined period of time are called "persistent" cookies.

How to delete cookies?

If you would prefer not to accept Cookies, most browsers will allow you to:

  1. change your browser settings to notify you when you receive a Cookie, which lets you choose whether or not to accept it;
  2. disable existing Cookies; or
  3. set your browser to automatically reject any Cookies.

Please be aware that if you disable or reject Cookies, some features and services on our Site may not work properly

IBM Digital Analytics. We use web analytics tools such as IBM Digital Analytics to assist us in understanding how people use our Services and related marketing and promotions. To manage your preferences with respect to IBM Digital Analytics click here.

Do Not Track. The CBMI website is not currently configured to recognize “do not track” signals sent as a header by Internet browsers. However, other settings within your browser may permit you to block cookies or other technology that enable websites to collect information that can be used to tailor advertisements to your interest. Please check your browser’s settings or consult your browser’s help information for more details. To learn more about browser tracking signals and Do Not Track please visit www.allaboutdnt.org.
